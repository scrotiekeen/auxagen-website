# Auxano Agency — Client Process Flow

**Internal reference document.**
The full pipeline from first contact to final handoff.

---

## Overview

Auxano runs every client through a defined pipeline. Each stage has a defined purpose, a defined output, and a defined transition into the next stage. No stage gets skipped. No stage gets blurred into the next.

Training Handoff is an **optional addon**, not a default inclusion. Clients who want long-term independence add it on. Clients who want Auxano to keep running their systems don't.

| Stage | Name | Cost to Client | Duration | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Free Consultation | $0 | 30–45 min | Qualify fit, identify the lever |
| 2 | Strategy Session | $250 | 60–75 min | Diagnose + present plans + close execution contract |
| 3 | Execution Contract | Variable | 1–6 months | Build and run the strategy with the client |
| 4 | Training Handoff *(optional addon)* | Variable | 2–4 weeks | Train the client's team to operate independently |
| 5 | Wrap-Up & Continuation | Variable | 30 days → ongoing | Close out, hand off operations, or transition to retainer |

## Stage 1: Free Consultation

### Purpose

Qualify the lead. Understand the business. Identify whether sales, marketing, or AI tooling (or a mix) is the right lever. End the meeting with a clear yes/no on whether to invite them to the Strategy Session.

### Cost

Free. 30–45 minutes. Video call or in-person.

### Who Runs It

Both partners on the call. Scrotie leads conversation, Carter observes and asks targeted follow-ups.

### What Happens

- Open with rapport — who they are, what the business does
- Discovery questions covering motive, current state, obstacles, and readiness
- Listen for the underlying issue beneath the surface complaint
- End with a clear next step: invite to Strategy Session, or politely decline

### Discovery Questions

- What does your business do, and who does it for?
- What made you start it?
- What does a good month look like right now? A bad one?
- What's the one thing, if solved, that would make the biggest difference?
- What have you already tried for that?
- Where are you spending time that you wish you weren't?
- What's held you back from moving on this sooner?

### Output

- Internal notes: diagnosis hypothesis, recommended plan direction, fit signal (1–5)
- Client receives: invitation to Strategy Session OR a polite no with optional 90-day follow-up

### Transition Criteria

Move to Stage 2 only if:

- The business has revenue or a clear path to revenue
- The owner can describe a real problem, not just a vague desire to "grow"
- The owner is willing to pay $250 for a paid diagnostic (commitment signal)
- Both partners agree the engagement would be worth Auxano's time

If any of those is missing, decline gracefully. Don't sell into a bad fit.

### What This Stage Is NOT

- Not a free deliverable. No plans given away.
- Not a pitch meeting. No selling the Strategy Session until the qualifying questions are answered.
- Not a therapy session. Stay on business.

## Stage 2: Strategy Session

### Purpose

Convert qualified leads into Execution Contracts. Diagnose the business, present a Big Plan and a Small Step Plan (both priced), and close on execution. Introduce the Training Handoff as an optional addon.

### Cost

$250 to the client. 60–75 minutes.

### Who Runs It

Both partners. Scrotie = empathy lead. Carter = direct lead. Roles are defined and held — see the Strategy Session Playbook for full meeting choreography.

### What Happens

The 48–72 hours before the meeting are prep. Both plans are documented and priced before walking in. Training Handoff is priced as a separate optional line item. The meeting itself runs in six phases:

1. **Opening (0–5 min):** Rapport, frame the agenda
2. **Diagnosis Reveal (5–15 min):** Specific, observable, rooted in client's own words
3. **Plan Reveal (15–35 min):** Big Plan only — the move, mechanics, research, outcome
4. **Discussional Feedback (35–45 min):** Listen for the branch signal
5. **Pricing Reveal (45–60 min):** Standalone value → with-execution price → Small Step alternative → Training Handoff addon
6. **Close (60–75 min):** Decision in the room, contract walked through

### Three Branches

- **Plan Feels Good:** Move directly to contract walkthrough
- **Plan Feels Too Big:** Reveal Small Step Plan as staircase, not retreat
- **Plan Doesn't Feel Good:** Diagnose objection type, decide whether to rework, defend, or walk away

### How to Position the Training Handoff Addon

Present it as a choice the client makes based on their long-term goal:

> *"There are two ways this engagement can end. Option one: we keep running it for you, ongoing. Option two: we add a Training Handoff at the end, where we train your team to take it over so you don't need us forever. Some clients want the long-term partnership. Some want independence. Both are real options — what fits where you're trying to go?"*

The addon is priced and scoped during the Strategy Session, not later. If the client wants to decide mid-Execution, that's allowed but framed as a contract amendment.

### Output

- Client receives: Strategy document with diagnosis, Big Plan ($), Small Step Plan ($), ROI projection (Conservative / Expected / Optimistic), execution contract draft, and Training Handoff addon pricing
- Auxano receives: signed contract OR clear no with reason

### Transition Criteria

Move to Stage 3 only when:

- Execution contract is signed
- Deposit (typically 50%) is paid
- Kickoff date is on the calendar
- Both parties have written scope agreement
- Training Handoff election (yes/no) is documented in the contract

### What This Stage Is NOT

- Not free advice. The $250 is paid before the meeting starts.
- Not a brainstorm. The plans are pre-built. The meeting is presentation + discussion + close.
- Not the money maker. The execution contract is.

## Stage 3: Execution Contract

### Purpose

Build and run the strategy alongside the client. This is where Auxano makes its money and where the work actually happens.

### Cost

Variable. Set during Strategy Session based on scope. Examples:

- Small Step engagement: $3,000–$5,000, 1–3 months
- Mid-scope engagement: $5,000–$12,000, 2–4 months
- Full build: $12,000+, 3–6 months

Payment structure: 50% deposit, balance over engagement timeline.

### Who Runs It

Both partners, with role splits based on scope. Examples:

- Sales execution → Carter leads outreach, Scrotie handles follow-up systems
- Marketing execution → Scrotie leads content and brand, Carter handles channel ops
- AI implementation → Whichever partner has the technical lean owns the build

### What Happens

- **Week 1 — Kickoff:** Re-confirm scope, gather access (accounts, brand assets, data), set communication cadence
- **Weeks 2 to N — Build & Run:** Auxano executes the plan. Weekly check-ins with the client. Mid-engagement adjustments based on real performance data.
- **Final 1–2 weeks — Wrap-Up Prep:** Document what was built. If Training Handoff was elected, identify which client team member(s) will take over which functions. If not, prepare ongoing operations plan or clean exit.

### Communication Cadence

- Weekly: Status email or short call
- Bi-weekly: Working session with client owner
- Mid-engagement: Formal review meeting with adjusted projections

### Output

Depends on scope. Always includes:

- Operational system or campaign that's running
- Documentation of what was built and how it works
- Performance data against the original ROI projections

If Training Handoff was elected, also includes:

- Identified handoff plan with named team members
- Defined competency or outcome standard for "trained"

### Transition Criteria

Move to Stage 4 (if Training Handoff elected) or Stage 5 (if not) when:

- Build phase is complete
- System or campaign is operational
- Performance baseline has been measured

### What This Stage Is NOT

- Not consulting. Auxano is doing the work, not advising on it.
- Not indefinite. Every contract has a defined end. No "we'll just keep going."
- Not solo. Client team is involved throughout, learning as Auxano executes.

## Stage 4: Training Handoff *(Optional Addon)*

### Purpose

Train the client's team to run what Auxano built. Only happens if the client elected the addon during the Strategy Session or amended their contract mid-Execution.

This is Auxano's differentiator. Most agencies skip this stage and keep clients dependent forever. Auxano makes it available — and makes the choice explicit.

### When This Stage Applies

Only when the client has elected the Training Handoff addon. If they didn't, skip directly to Stage 5.

### Cost

Priced separately as a contract addon. Not included in the base Execution Contract. Pricing depends on scope of what's being handed off — typical range $1,500–$5,000 depending on system complexity and number of team members trained.

### Who Runs It

Whichever partner led the relevant function during Execution. Both partners present at the final handoff session.

### What Happens

- **Session 1 — Walkthrough:** Live demo of the system or campaign. Client team takes notes, asks questions.
- **Session 2 — Shadow:** Client team member runs the system with Auxano observing. Auxano corrects in real time.
- **Session 3 — Solo:** Client team runs it alone. Auxano reviews after.
- **Final session — Sign-off:** Written playbook delivered. Q&A. Defined competency confirmed.

### Competency Definition

Before training starts, both sides agree on what "trained" means. Two options:

- **Competency-based:** Client team can perform a defined checklist of tasks unsupervised
- **Outcome-based:** Client team hits a specific metric within a defined window

Pick one per engagement. Document it. Both sides sign off when met.

### Output

- Written playbook (PDF or shared doc) with every step documented
- Trained team members named and certified
- Defined boundary: what Auxano handles vs. what client now owns

### Transition Criteria

Move to Stage 5 when:

- All training sessions complete
- Playbook delivered and reviewed
- Client team has demonstrated competency or hit the agreed outcome
- Final invoice paid

### What This Stage Is NOT

- Not default. It's an addon. Most clients won't take it on first engagement.
- Not a slide deck. It's hands-on, sequential, and ends with the client running it alone.
- Not a discount on Execution. It's additive scope, additive price.

## Stage 5: Wrap-Up & Continuation

### Purpose

Close out the active engagement and decide what comes next. Three possible outcomes: clean exit, ongoing operations retainer, or new Execution Contract for a different scope.

### Cost

Depends on what the client elects. The wrap-up itself is included in the original Execution Contract.

### Who Runs It

Whichever partner led the engagement.

### What Happens

The path through Stage 5 depends on whether the client elected the Training Handoff addon.

#### Path A — Training Handoff Was Elected

The client's team is now running the system. Auxano's role shrinks to:

- 30-day light support window included (email-first, 1 business day response)
- Optional advisory retainer at the end of the 30 days
- Clean close if no retainer is signed

The client owns operations. Auxano stays available for strategic questions and edge cases.

#### Path B — Training Handoff Was NOT Elected

The system is built and running, but no one on the client side has been trained to operate it independently. The client now decides:

- **Option 1: Operations Retainer.** Auxano keeps running the system at a defined monthly rate. This is the default path when handoff wasn't elected.
- **Option 2: Add Training Handoff Now.** Client amends the contract and moves into Stage 4. Pricing is the same as if elected upfront.
- **Option 3: Clean Close.** Client takes the system over without training. Auxano documents what was built and exits. This is rare and usually a sign the engagement ended badly.

The Operations Retainer is the most common Path B outcome — it's why the addon model works. Clients who want long-term partnership get it. Clients who want independence pay extra for the training that gets them there.

### The Retainer Conversation

Don't soft-pitch. State the offer clearly:

- What the retainer covers (defined scope)
- What it costs (defined monthly rate)
- What happens if they say no (clean exit OR add Training Handoff)

For Path A clients, retainers are advisory. For Path B clients, retainers are operational. Different scopes, different pricing, different conversations.

### Output

- Final performance report against original ROI projections
- Decision documented: clean close, retainer, or new contract
- Case study material gathered (with permission)

### Transition

The engagement closes when one of the following happens:

- Path A: 30 days pass and client opts out of retainer (clean close)
- Path A: Client signs advisory retainer (ongoing)
- Path B: Client signs operations retainer (ongoing)
- Path B: Client elects Training Handoff (move to Stage 4)
- Either path: Client signs a new Execution Contract for different scope (return to Stage 3)

### What This Stage Is NOT

- Not unlimited support. Defined boundaries on whichever path.
- Not a billable hours arrangement. Flat-rate retainers or nothing.
- Not the end of the relationship. Clients can come back at any time for new scopes.

## Pipeline Summary

```
Lead Source
   ↓
[Stage 1] Free Consultation (30–45 min, $0)
   ↓ qualified
[Stage 2] Strategy Session (60–75 min, $250)
   ↓ contract signed (with or without Training Handoff addon)
[Stage 3] Execution Contract (1–6 months, $3K–$15K+)
   ↓ build complete
   ├── Handoff elected → [Stage 4] Training Handoff (2–4 weeks, addon pricing)
   │                        ↓
   │                     [Stage 5 — Path A] Wrap-Up
   │                        → Clean close OR advisory retainer
   │
   └── Handoff not elected → [Stage 5 — Path B] Wrap-Up
                                → Operations retainer (most common)
                                → Add Training Handoff late
                                → Clean close (rare)
```

## Internal Principles

A few things that hold across every stage:

1. **No stage is free that should be paid.** Free Consultation is the only free stage. Every plan, document, or deliverable beyond it is paid.
2. **Training Handoff is sold, not given.** Make it explicit in the Strategy Session. Don't bake the cost into Execution and pretend it's free — that hides the value.
3. **Every stage has a defined exit.** Nothing drags. No "we'll just keep going."
4. **The choice between handoff and ongoing retainer is the client's.** Both are legitimate. Auxano's pitch is "you decide whether you want to be independent or partnered — we'll do either."
5. **Walk away from bad fits early.** Stage 1 is the cheapest place to say no. Stage 5 is the most expensive. The earlier you decline, the more time you save for clients who are right.
